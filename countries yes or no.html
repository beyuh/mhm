Countries: Yes or No?

Top Gay-Friendly Countries in Asia

Rank Country Reason
1 Taiwan
  • Became the first country in Asia to pass equal marriage laws (passed in May 2019).
  • Homosexuality was never illegal.
  • Have Taipei Pride (October) and the WOOW Pool Party and the Formusa Pride Party, and monthly Blush queer parties.
2 Thailand
  • 88.72% of Thais accept gays.
  • Homosexuality was legalised (1956).
  • Large gay scenes in Bangkok, Phuket, and Chiang Mai.
  • Have the Songkran Bangkok Gay Circuit Party (April), the Asia Circuit Festival (June), and the BangkokThai International Film Festival (BANGIFF) (October)
3 Cambodia
  • Has an unexpectedly large gay scene in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap.
  • Homosexuality was never illegal.
  • Has the Declaration of Family Relationship (DoFR), which is a public ceremony for gay couples (2018).
4 Japan
  • Homosexuality was legalised (1880).
  • Has a large gay scene in Tokyo and few queer hangouts in Nagasaki, Osaka, Hiroshima, and Tokyo.
  • Has Tokyo Rainbow Pride (April/May) and the Rainbow Reel Tokyo LGBTQ film festival (July).
5 The Philippines
  • 73% of Filipinos felt that homosexuality should be accepted. It is still, however, influenced by the Catholic Church with very homophobic views.
  • Homosexuality was never illegal.
  • The main gay scene is in Manila, but there are also some in Davao City, Quezon City, Cebu City, and Boracay.
  • Has Manila Pride (June), Quezon City Pride (March), and the QC International Pink Film Festival (QCIPFF)(November)
6 Hong Kong
  • Homosexuality was legalised (1991).
  • Has a large gay scene in Hong Kong with several bars, clubs and parties.
  • Has Hong Kong Pride (November), an LGBTQ Film Festival (September), and an annual procession for International Day Against Homophobia, Biphobia, Intersexism and Transphobia (IDAHOBIT).
7 Seoul
  • Seoul has one of the best and biggest gay scenes in Asia.
  • Has the second largest gay festival and a cultural hub for queer culture.
  • Homosexuality was never illegal.
  • Has Seoul Drag Parade (May), the Seoul Queer Festival (July), the Korea Queer Film Festival (July), and the I am Seoul circuit parties (August).
8 Vietnam
  • Has progressing LGBTQ laws.
  • Lacks a big gay scene compared to South Korea and Hong Kong.
  • Homosexuality was never illegal.
  • Gay wedding ceremonies are allowed but not legally recognised and do not offer any rights.
  • Has a small gay scene in Saigon and few gay hangouts in Hanoi and Da Nang.
  • Has Viet Pride (August)
9 India
  • Has the potential to become a big gay mecca in Asia.
  • Homosexuality was legalised (2018)
  • More and more gay hangouts are opening up in Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore.
  • Has the Mumbai Pride (Queer Azaadi Mumbai) and the Mr Gay India pageant (January/February), Chennai Pride (June), the Kashish Mumbai Internation Queer Film Festival (June), Bangalore Pride (October/November), and the Kolkata Rainbow Pride Walk (December).
10 Nepal
  • Nepalese society remains conservative, with men expected to marry and have children.
  • Has a comparatively small gay scene.
  • Homosexuality was legalised (2007).
  • Has some straight places that have a gay night on weekends in Kathmandu.
  • Has Mr Gay Handsome beauty pageant (June) and the annual Gaijatra LGBTQ Pride Parade in Kathmandu (usually August).
